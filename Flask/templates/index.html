Our Story

What is YogaLens?

✦

Yoga is a 5000-year-old practice developed in ancient India by the Indus-Sarasvati civilization. The word yoga means deep association and union of mind with the body. It keeps both mind and body in equilibrium through asana, meditation, and several other techniques.

Nowadays, yoga has gained worldwide attention due to increased stress levels in the modern lifestyle. While yoga can be practised in centres or with personal tutors, many people prefer self-learning — but in self-learning, incorrect posture can be harmful to health, resulting in acute pain and long-term chronic concerns.

YogaLens bridges that gap. Upload a photo and our Xception-based model instantly identifies your pose, giving you the confidence to practise safely on your own.

Inception V3

48 layers · 299×299 input
Input: 299×299×3
→ Convolution layers
→ Inception modules (factorised convolutions)
→ Average Pooling → Dropout
→ Fully Connected → Softmax
Output: 8×8×2048 → 1001 classes

InceptionV3 is a 48-layer deep convolutional neural network developed by Google. It improves on earlier Inception versions by factorising large convolutions into smaller, asymmetric ones — reducing computation while keeping accuracy high. The pretrained network can classify images into 1000 object categories and is widely used as a base for transfer learning tasks in medical imaging, wildlife detection, and pose recognition.

Xception

Used in YogaLens · 299×299 input
Input: 299×299×3
→ Entry Flow (conv + depthwise separable conv)
→ Middle Flow × 8 (repeated residual blocks)
→ Exit Flow
→ Global Average Pooling
→ Dense(5, Softmax) — 5 yoga pose classes

Xception (Extreme Inception) was introduced by François Chollet at Google in 2017. It replaces standard Inception modules with depthwise separable convolutions — performing channel-wise and spatial convolutions independently — which makes it both more accurate and more parameter-efficient than InceptionV3. This is the model powering YogaLens, fine-tuned on yoga pose data to classify five poses: Downward Dog, Goddess, Plank, Tree, and Warrior II.

ResNet 50

50 layers · 224×224 input
Input: 224×224×3
→ Conv1 (7×7, stride 2) → MaxPool
→ Residual Blocks × 16 (skip connections)
→ Global Average Pooling
→ Fully Connected → Softmax (1000 classes)

ResNet-50 (Residual Network) was introduced by Microsoft Research in 2015 and won the ImageNet competition that year. Its key innovation is the residual (skip) connection — a shortcut that lets gradients bypass one or more layers, solving the vanishing gradient problem that made very deep networks hard to train. With 50 layers and ~25 million parameters, it strikes a strong balance between depth and trainability, making it one of the most widely used backbones in computer vision research.

VGG 19

19 layers · 224×224 input
Input: 224×224×3
→ 16 Convolutional Layers (all 3×3 filters)
→ 5 Max Pooling Layers
→ 3 Fully Connected Layers (4096 → 4096 → 1000)
→ Softmax (1000 classes)

VGG19, developed by the Visual Geometry Group at Oxford, proved that network depth using very small (3×3) convolution filters is a critical component for achieving strong performance. Its simplicity and uniformity made it easy to understand and adapt. Despite having ~143 million parameters (making it quite heavy), it remains widely used as a feature extractor in transfer learning, style transfer, and as a perceptual loss network in image generation tasks.
